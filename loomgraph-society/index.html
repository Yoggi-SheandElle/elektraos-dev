A Hub . Not a Tool

Loomgraph Society

A hub of working websites that grow together. Shared SEO audits. Shared briefs. Contextual, editorial cross-links woven between real businesses by agents, approved by humans, shipped through every CMS on the stack.

The pact

One site cannot rank alone unless it is the loudest. A hub of connected sites can rank together by being the most useful. The Loomgraph Society is built on that premise: every member runs its own business, keeps its own voice, owns its traffic. Loomgraph - the agentic platform underneath - handles the unglamorous shared work: audits, briefs, internal-link proposals, IndexNow pings, structured data. Nothing goes live without a human approving it. Nothing that violates Google Search Essentials, FTC disclosure, GDPR, or the member's own editorial standards ever gets proposed a second time.

How the hub grows, legally

Domain authority compounds when every member publishes something genuinely useful and the rest of the hub links to it only where the topic actually warrants it. The agents propose; humans approve. Every mechanic below stays inside Google Search Essentials, FTC disclosure rules, and GDPR/CCPA consent boundaries.

01Topic-cluster weaving

Each member owns authority topics (MealShift owns agent-native commerce, SheAndElle owns heritage DTC, elektraos.dev owns AI-systems build logs). When a new post on any member touches someone else's authority topic, the Weaver agent proposes a contextual link. Human approves or rejects.

02Schema.org sameAs + author affiliation

Every member carries Person and Organization JSON-LD with sameAs pointing across members and to the founder's verified LinkedIn, GitHub, and ORCID. Search engines and LLMs see a real, disclosed affiliation graph - not a hidden PBN.

03Editorial byline exchange

Real author-level contributions: when a subject-matter expert from one member writes a guest piece for another, it appears with the author's real byline, bio, and rel=author. No ghost-written filler. No exchange of arbitrary links for arbitrary links.

04Shared research, cited not copied

Loomgraph's Strategist agent produces research briefs from public data (PageSpeed, CrUX, GSC, Common Crawl). Members cite the brief URL on elektraos.dev when they use the data. One original source, many contextual references.

05IndexNow, not link injection

When a member publishes, Loomgraph pings IndexNow so Bing, Yandex, and Seznam re-crawl within minutes. No automated link drops on third-party sites. No scraped blog-comment spam.

06Real press, real mentions

HARO-style internal expert sourcing: when a member writes a post needing a quote, the society's experts are surfaced first. When a real journalist asks, members respond with real attributable expertise. No paid placements undisclosed.

07Approval queue, human in the loop

Every cross-link proposal, every content brief, every on-site edit lands in an approval queue. Nothing ships to a member's CMS without explicit human approval from that member's owner. Auditable.

08Shared audit infrastructure

PageSpeed, CrUX, sitemap crawl, internal-link graph, orphan-page detection - every member gets the same free audit loop that ElektraOS runs on itself. Costs go down for everyone. Data stays in the member's account.

What the society refuses to do

Forbidden mechanics (auto-rejected by the approval queue)

  • Paid link schemes or link exchanges without disclosure (violates Google Search Essentials)
  • Private blog networks or hidden-ownership link farms
  • Automated comment spam, forum injection, or scraped-content posting
  • Doorway pages, cloaking, sneaky redirects, or user-agent switching
  • AI-generated thin content published without human editorial review
  • Tracking cookies or fingerprinting without GDPR/CCPA consent
  • Affiliate links without rel=sponsored and FTC-compliant disclosure
  • Any mechanic a member cannot defend in a Google Search Central forum post
